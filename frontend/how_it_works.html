How ResumeAI Works

Understand exactly how your resume is scored and what you can do to land more interviews.

The 5 Scoring Modules

Keyword Score 30%

Checks overlap with industry keywords and job description terms. This ensures you're using the technical vocabulary that recruiters and ATS filters look for.

Achievement Score 25%

Looks for action verbs and quantified results (%, $, numbers). We prioritize bullet points that demonstrate impact rather than just listing responsibilities.

ATS Score 20%

Checks formatting, section headings, and word count (target: 300-1400). We flag special characters or complex layouts that might break automated parsers.

Soft Skills Score 15%

Detects 19 key soft skill terms like leadership, teamwork, and communication. These are critical for cultural fit and behavioral screening.

Completeness Score 10%

Ensures your resume has all essential parts: Summary, Experience, Education, Skills, and Contact sections.

Grade Thresholds

Your final score determines your overall grade

0-54 Poor 55-69 Needs Work 70-84 Good 85-100 Excellent

Semantic Match (RAG)

When you paste a job description, ResumeAI goes beyond simple keyword matching. We use AI embeddings (FAISS) to measure the semantic similarity between your experience and the job requirements. This captures your "fit" even if you use different wording than the JD.

AI Suggestions

Powered by the Groq LLM, we generate personalized feedback for every screening. You'll get concrete rewrites for weak bullet points, identified keyword gaps, and actionable "Quick Wins" categorized by priority levels.

How to Get the Best Score

  • Keep your word count between 300 and 1400 words.
  • Use strong action verbs (e.g., "Led", "Optimized", "Scaled") and avoid passive language.
  • Quantify your achievements—use percentages, dollar amounts, and headcount numbers.
  • Always paste the job description to unlock the deep semantic matching analysis.
